Rizal’s Education

Posted on July 4, 2012 by marveylu

Rizal’s Education

Teodora Alonso, Jose Rizal’s mother, was the first teacher Rizal had. She taught him how to read, pray, and encouraged him in to write poetry. She wanted him to pursue on writing because it reduces the monotony of memorizing and to stimulate Rizal’s imagination.

As Rizal grew, he had some private tutors to enrich his knowledge. His first private tutor was Maestro Calestino. The next was Maestro Lucas Padua. Then came Leon Monroy who lived with Rizal and taught Rizal in Spanish and Latin. Although after five month with Leon Monroy, his tutor passes away, and Rizal’s parents decided to send him to school in Binan.

Binan

Jose left Calamba in June 1868 for Binan with the accompaniment of his brother, Paciano. They proceeded to their aunt’s home in where they lodged as they arrived. Rizal was sent to school at Maestro Justiniano Aquino Cruz’s home. It was only a small nipa hut about 30 meters away from his aunt’s house.

Rizal excelled in his his academics during his stay there. He surpasses most of his elder classmates in Spanish, Latin, and more of their subjects. Some of his classmates grew jelous of him and started telling lies about Rizal to discredit him which led to the teacher to reprimand him.

Although Rizal’s stay in Binan wasn’t all about studying, he also had the opportunity to attend mass, drew sketches, and make friends with other boys there. He only stayed in Binan for a year and a half.

Ateneo

Rizal entered Ateneo de Municipal in 1872 wherein he encountered his first professor, Fr. Jose Bech. On his second year there, he also had the same professors during his first year. He recided at No. 6 Calle Magallanes and received remarkable grades in every class he had and received a gold medal by the end of the term. Jose also devoted most of his time to read novels. He also included romantic novels which helped him to enrich his creative view in writing.

On his third year in June 1874, he was surprised by his mother, whom has gotten out of prison. This motivated him to pursue on studying and remained on top of his class. He had a written a lot of poems then up until his fourth year in Ateneo. He also made sure to engage in physical education to improve his small built. He attended gymnastics and fencing. But still, he gave time for his painting and sculpting. He graduated in the Ateneo as Sobresaliente or “outstanding”, which correspond as the highest attainable recognition in the Ateneo.

UST

Rizal pursued his studies at the University of Santo Tomas after graduating in Ateneo. He first took up a year studying Philosophy and Letters mainly because his father wanted him to take that course and he was still uncertain what career he would pursue, but he the decided to shift to a medical course to be a physician to cure his mother’s failing eyesight and it was also recommended by Fr. Pablo Ramon, the Father Rector of Ateneo. During his time as a Thomasian, Jose still remained loyal to Ateneo. He took up a course in land surveying and continued to participate in extracurricular activities there. Although he won more literary laurels and had more romances with girls there.

He finished his surveying course in Ateneo in 1878 as Expert surveyor. He then took his pre-medical course at the same time with his medical course which in normal circumstances is not allowed. Jose was one of only four who was granted that privilege of 28. He did his four years of practical training in medicine at the Hospital de San Juan de Dios in Intramuros. Rizal attained the grade of Notable(very good) in every subject and was second best in the course.

Rizal departed for Spain onMay 3, 1882 on Salvadora that is bound for Singapore. He kept this from friars, Spanish authorities, and even his family. He used Jose Mercado as his name to avoid detection. Jose enrolled in Universidad Central de Madrid taking up two courses: Philosophy and Letters, and Medicine. OnJune 21, 1884, he consulted the degree of Liciente in Medicine then passed every subject the following year leading to the degree of Doctor of Medicine. Although he wasn’t awarded his Doctor’s Diploma because he was unfortunate not to be able to submit his thesis personally nor paid the corresponding fees.

Other than his medical degree, Jose also finished Philosophy and Letters with high grades. He was awarded the Degree of Liciente in Philosophy and Letters by the Universidad Central de Madrid with the rating of excellent on June 19, 1885.

Rizal went to Paris to continue learning more about ophthalmology. He worked there as an assistant to Dr. Louis de Weckert, a leading French ophthalmologist.

He then went on to Heidelberg on February 3, 1886 after having some experience in ophthalmology. He worked at the University Eye Hospital under the direction of Dr. Otto Becker who is a well known German ophthalmologist. It was also in Heidelgerg where he wrote his poem A Las Flores de Heidelberg.

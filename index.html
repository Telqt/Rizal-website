Who is Jose Rizal?

José Protacio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda was born on June 19, 1861 in Calamba, Laguna. The seventh of eleven children born to a relatively well-off family in a Dominican-owned tenant land in Calamba, Laguna. Jose Rizal lived and died during the Spanish colonial era in the Philippines

José Rizal, the Philippine national hero, was a prolific writer who produced a significant amount of literary works.

Noli Me Tángere (Touch Me Not) (1887) - Rizal's most famous novel that exposes the social injustices, corruption, abuse of power by the Spanish friars, and the dire situation of the Philippines under Spanish rule.

El Filibusterismo (The Reign of Greed) (1891) - considered a sequel to Noli, is a darker novel that portrays a revolution brewing as a response to the oppressive conditions.

Mi último adiós (My Last Farewell) (1896) - a poem written by Rizal the night before his execution, expressing his love for his country, his family, and his friends.
